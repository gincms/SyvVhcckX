v1.0.0

Since the first version of InterTrader (formerly Cryptocurrency Exchange Comparison) was released on Google Play, a lot of work has gone into refining and enhancing it to so that it does as much as possible to simplify and speed up the process of finding solid intertrades.

As mentioned in the introduction to intertrading, I've spent a great deal of time (and some crypto) using intertrader to trade, in order to figure the challenges and pitfalls, and then build features into the app that reduce or eliminate them completely. The result is the addition of the following new features in v1.0.0:

  • Profit analysis across 5000+ exchange and trade pair combinations
  • Analysis result filters to refine results
  • Orderbooks of exchanges available directly within the app
  • Cost calculator accessible on comaprison and orderbook pages
  • Clear indicators on comparison page when profit is available
  • Names of coins on exchanges added (where available) to reduce possible coin-clash
  • Removal of unreliable/limited exchanges
  • Addition of new exchanges
  • Menu for quick access to guides and online support

Profit analysis

Originally, InterTrader showed you a simple comparison of the prices of a cryptocurrency on different exchanges. While this was useful, to find potentially profitable pairs (or 3Ps, for short) you had to manually assess each trading pair. In v1.0.0 InterTrader does all the calculation for you and returns a list of 3Ps, and even indicates if there are wallet issues that may affect your trade. This is a massive time-saver and makes it much easier to find profit.

Analysis results filters

Along with the profit analysis, InterTrader also allows you to refine your results. Using the search filters, you can set the minimum profit you'd like, adjust the maximum buy/sell spread, increase the minimum number of exchanges listing a trade pair, and more. Check out the guide to refining your search results for more info.

Orderbooks

One of the time-consuming parts of intertrading is the process of checking if the exchange has sufficient supply/demand to fulfill a profitable intertrade. This has been spead up by the ability to view the orderbooks for exchanges directly from the comparison page.

Cost calculator

While using InterTrader I often found myself wanting to calculate the value of a specific quantity of a coin. Sometimes I wanted to see how much I needed to purchase, or if the amount in the orderbooks would cover my costs. I spent a lot of time with a separate calculator open, or doing the calculations on the exchanges. To save time and avoid the back and forth, InterTrader now includes a calculator that can be quickly accessed on the comparison and orderbook screens.

Profit indicators

Visual cues have been added to the comparison page to make it obvious when there's potential profit to be made from a trade pair. The most profitable buy and sell prices are presented in a LARGER and bolder text. You'll also see the in the header bar, which you can tap to view the trade pair's profit analysis.

Coin names

Although it doesn't happen too often, I came across a few instances where coins shared the same ticker symbol. To reduce ambiguity, The coin names used on each exchange is presented whenever that information is available. Make sure you pay attention to the coin names (and check for yourself) to avoid a bad intertrade as a result of "coin-clash".

Better quality exchange data

During development and testing of InterTrader, it became clear just how much the quality of data available from exchanges varied. There were also other unhealthy signals that meant certain exchanges had to be removed. But InterTrader now has even more exchanges, with the addition of: Bit-Z, Gate.IO and Kucoin.
