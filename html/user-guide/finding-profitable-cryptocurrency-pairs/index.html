Finding profitable cryptocurrency pairs

Using search and analysis to find profitable trades

InterTrader sorts through a lot of data to find you the most profitable opportunities. The outcome of its analysis is mostly presented on the direct comparison page for each market pair, and the search/analysis results page. Between these two sections, you'll find a lot of the key information you need. This page goes into more detail about the analysis results page. If you're looking for more general information, try the quick start guide.

Analysis

Tap the Compare tab to go to the Analysis results. InterTrader will automatically search for profitable trades using default search criteria.

If no results are found, it may be because it hasn't yet retrieved the data from the exchanges. Swipe down on the screen to refresh its data. This can a minute, but once done analysis is very quick.

Tips to make the most of the analysis results

It's important to pay attention to the data provided for each item in the search results, as there are often indicators that may make it difficult, or even impossible, to capitalise on the price differences.

Warnings

If there's an exclamation icon, or the profit is greyed out, make sure you click through to the direct comparison page to find out which exchanges have issues and if they'll affect your trade.

Excessive buy/sell price differences or unusually high potential profit

When there are large differences between the lowest and highest buy/sell prices, or significantly high profit can indicate that something is significantly skewing the figures. Causes can be, but aren't limited to:

  • Two different coins are being compared because of coin clash (two or more coins sharing the same ticker symbol)
  • There's a problem with the coin or trade pair on an exchange, causing the price to deviate significantly from the average of the other exchanges
  • The price is being manipulated on one of the exchanges (e.g. someone setting an inexplicably high buy price, usually for a very small quantity)

Check the direct comparison page and review the orderbooks for exchanges of interest.

Update time

This is easily overlooked, but time passes quickly while trading and you can easily find yourself looking at results based on prices that are an hour old. Swipe down on the screen to refresh all the price data.

Remember that the price update can take a minute, depending on your connection, so don't refresh too often.
